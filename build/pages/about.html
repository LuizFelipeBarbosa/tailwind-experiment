About Us

This film is a collaboration between Critical Resistance, founded 25 years ago to advance an abolitionist understanding of the prison industrial complex (PIC), and activists from Detention Watch Network, composed of more than 100 immigrant rights organizations, Freedom for Immigrants, dedicated to abolishing immigrant detention, and California Immigrant Youth Justice Alliance and Inland Coalition for Immigrant Justice.

Mayon Denton

DIRECTOR & PRODUCER

Mayon Denton (he/him) is a film and television producer and director, born and raised in LA. After graduating from UCLA, Mayon has produced more than 10 feature films, TV pilots, music videos, industrial videos and commercials. Mayon is known for Distortion (2017) Surviving... (2015), and Against the Grain (2012). His last film, Second Team was nominated for the 2022 Cannes Film Festival Emerging Filmmaker Showcase.

Cinthya Martinez

Producer & Writer

Cinthya Martinez (she/her) is a first-generation Chicana from Mexican immigrant parents and now living in the San Gabriel Valley. Raised by a single undocumented mother she writes about migrant feminist abolition praxis. She became an activist in high school involved in the Immigrant Youth Coalition- SCV and is a researcher invested in abolishing ICE and border-prisons. This film is based on her life and dissertation, ICE on Fire.

Setsu Shigematsu

DIRECTOR, PRODUCER & WRITER

Setsu Shigematsu (xi, xer, xem) is a feminist-abolitionist media-maker and the director of Visions of Abolition: From Critical Resistance to a New Way of Life (2011) featuring Angela Y. Davis, Ruth Wilson Gilmore, and Susan Burton. Setsu is a member of Critical Resistance Abolitionist Educators and an Associate Professor of Media & Cultural Studies at the University of California, Riverside.

This film is a collaboration with Layla Razavi of Freedom For Immigrants, Marcela Hernandez of Detention Watch Network, Lizbeth Abeln of Inland Coalition for Immigrant Justice, Mitize Perez and Berto Hernandez of California Immigrant Youth Justice Alliance. Narrated by Yessica Gonzales, a leading activist in the Abolish ICE movement in California.
